Egg & Offspring Progression System

OTAB treats eggs, offspring and adult creatures as connected stages of one larger progression system.

Instead of always going directly from item to adult creature, OTAB allows creatures to pass through intermediate stages such as eggs, growing forms or ritual-based transformation steps.

Why This System Exists

In vanilla Valheim, creature progression is usually simple: an egg grows, hatches, and produces a clear result.

OTAB expands this idea by allowing some creatures to follow more complex progression paths.

This makes it possible to create:

  • indirect taming paths
  • ritual-based creature acquisition
  • multi-stage growth
  • lore-driven creature progression

Vanilla Rules Are Still Respected

Whenever possible, OTAB keeps Valheim's familiar egg and growth behavior intact.

This includes things such as:

  • fire requirements
  • roof requirements
  • cover checks
  • growth timers
  • normal hatch conditions

If an OTAB egg uses only vanilla-style rules, it behaves the way players would normally expect.

OTAB extends these mechanics, but tries to keep them understandable and consistent with the original game.

Multi-Stage Progression

Some OTAB creatures do not progress directly from egg to adult.

Instead, a creature may pass through several stages before reaching its final form.

For example, an egg may hatch into:

  • another egg-like stage
  • a growing offspring
  • an intermediate item

This allows OTAB to represent transformations, awakenings and layered progression paths that would be difficult to express with only one simple hatch timer.

Ritual Eggs and Player Involvement

Some eggs in OTAB are designed as part of a ritual or special progression path.

These eggs may require more than simply placing them down and waiting.

Depending on the design, progression may involve:

  • player presence
  • repeated interaction
  • multiple hatch or growth stages

This differs from normal vanilla eggs, where players can place an egg, leave the area, and return later to find the result.

In OTAB, ritual-based progression is meant to remain intentional instead of becoming a passive background process.

Unquiet Items

During development, OTAB naturally introduced a class of items that behave like unstable or incomplete eggs.

These are sometimes referred to as unquiet items.

They are not fully developed creatures, but they are also more than ordinary items. Instead, they represent transitional states such as:

  • dormant entities
  • interrupted transformations
  • incomplete awakenings

In gameplay terms, they often act like one step in a longer creature progression path.

Offspring as Their Own Stage

In OTAB, offspring are not treated as miniature adults.

They are their own gameplay stage and can behave differently from the final adult creature.

Depending on the creature, an offspring may:

  • be tameable even if the adult is not
  • require feeding or care
  • grow into a different final creature
  • remain permanently in its juvenile form

Example: Indirect Abomination Taming

A good example of this system is the Abomination.

In OTAB, the adult Abomination is not simply made tameable. Instead, the player must go through an indirect progression path.

This path involves:

  • creating a ritual egg
  • hatching that egg into a growing offspring form
  • taming the growing form before it matures

This keeps the adult creature special while still allowing players to eventually obtain a tamed version through effort and progression.

Visual Design Choices

OTAB generally avoids changing original creature models purely for progression stages.

Instead, visual differentiation is usually handled through icons and other lightweight presentation changes.

This helps keep assets:

  • compatible with other mods
  • visually consistent
  • easy to extend later

Why This Matters

Eggs and offspring in OTAB are not shortcuts.

They exist to make creature progression feel more deliberate, more readable, and more connected to the creature.

The goal is to create progression that players can observe, understand and actively take part in — rather than something that always happens automatically in the background.
